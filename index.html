Research Profile

Overview

I am a computational genomics and molecular biology researcher with strong dual expertise in dry-lab bioinformatics and wet-lab experimentation. My research focuses on host–pathogen interactions, small RNA–mediated gene regulation, three-dimensional genome architecture and genome evolution in oomycetes and fungi.

My work combines next-generation sequencing — Nanopore, PacBio, Illumina and Hi-C — with advanced bioinformatics pipelines, including chromosome-scale genome assemblies, comparative genomics, transcriptomics, 3D-genomics and regulatory network analysis. In parallel, I apply wet-lab approaches such as RNA-seq validation, gene expression analysis, protein and redox assays, pathogen infection assays and transgenic plant studies to experimentally validate computational discoveries.

Through an SERB- and UGC-funded fellowship spanning over seven years, I have developed an integrated, systems-level understanding of host–pathogen interactions, particularly in Phytophthora–plant systems. My long-term goal is to use genome-driven biology to design next-generation strategies for crop disease resistance and sustainable agriculture.

Positions & Experience

Research

Current Position

Senior Research Fellow (SRF)

Jan 2021 – Present

CSIR – Indian Institute of Chemical Biology, Kolkata

Doctoral research: Unravelling Phytophthora capsici pathogenesis via three-dimensional genome architecture and multi-omics approaches.

  • Identification and confirmation of pathogenic Phytophthora capsici isolates from southern Asia
  • Comparative genomics across Phytophthora species
  • Whole-genome sequencing of P. capsici using Illumina, Oxford Nanopore and Hi-C to generate a complete chromosome-scale genome assembly
  • Resolving the three-dimensional genome architecture of this pathogenic oomycete, including its chromatin organization and regulatory complexity
  • Generating high-quality genome assemblies for host tomato (Pusa Rubi) and chili (local variety) using PacBio sequencing
  • Transcriptomic profiling across P. capsici life stages and dual RNA-seq of infected hosts (tomato and chili) to map host–microbe interaction networks
  • Identifying and characterizing oomycete effector repertoires and their role in establishing infection
